The early Web was full of so many great things. It had character, being full of hardcore nerds making websites. Not many people truly knew what they were doing yet, and that's probably what made it so great. It was like the Web fostered a "culture" that ascended geopolitical borders.

Contrast this with the Internet of the 90s and 2000s, and I would hope you'll see we have lost much of the character that used to inhabit these lands. It used to mostly consist of personal sites, lovingly hand-made with the holy trinity of Web design. Now, everything seems like it's just some corporation. Articles suck, some borderline impossible to read without an adblocker. And everybody is at eachother's throats; not that such attitudes haven't been around on the net since its inception, but it seems like now it's worse than ever.

Despite not personally living through what I see as the best period of the Internet (I was born in 2004), I have a penchant for the aesthetics and community of that time, and my interest in Web development originates there.

Funnily enough, I don't believe the oldweb is dead. I think it's just a little deeper than the Facebooks and Amazons of today. Websites like Neocities are basically entirely devoted to the continuation of what the net once was. Plenty of people are still hosting their cool and unique websites, often with the aesthetics of the oldweb.

Pipertext is dedicated to pooling all of the best resources related to Web 1.0, whether that be modern imitations (such as this site!) or actual stuff from way back in the day; pages for your browsing pleasure, but also resources for making those websites.
